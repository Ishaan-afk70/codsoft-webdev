Bhagat Singh

The Unsung Hero of Indian Independence

A Brief Introduction

Bhagat Singh (1907-1931) was a charismatic Indian socialist revolutionary whose two acts of dramatic violence against the British in India and execution at age 23 made him a folk hero of the Indian independence movement.

Key Contributions

Revolutionary Activities

Bhagat Singh was involved in several revolutionary activities, including the bombing of the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi.

Ideological Influence

He was instrumental in popularizing the phrase "Inquilab Zindabad" (Long Live the Revolution) which became a slogan of the Indian independence movement.

Martyrdom

His execution by the British colonial authorities at the age of 23 inspired many young people to join the freedom struggle.

Timeline of Bhagat Singh's Life

1907

Born in Banga, Punjab

1923

1925

Became a member of the Hindustan Republican Association

1928

Founded the Naujawan Bharat Sabha

1929

1931

Executed by hanging in Lahore Central Jail

Legacy

Bhagat Singh's legacy continues to inspire generations of Indians. His revolutionary zeal, courage, and intellectual prowess have earned him an iconic status in Indian history. Despite his short life, his impact on the Indian independence movement was profound and far-reaching.

Today, Bhagat Singh is remembered not just as a freedom fighter, but as a symbol of the fight against injustice and oppression. His life and sacrifice continue to be celebrated through various books, films, and memorials across India.
